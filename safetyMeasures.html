Wash Your Hands

×

How long should I wash my hands?

Wash your hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds. You can also use hand sanitizer if soap and water is not available.

When should I wash my hands?

- Before eating or making food

- After leaving a public place

- After touching your mask

- After blowing your nose, coughing, or sneezing

- After spending time with someone sick

- After using the restroom

Avoid Touching Your Face

×

How do you get the virus?

The virus enters your body through your eyes, nose, and mouth. In your body, it makes a lot of copies of itself. When there are too many copies of the virus, you get sick.

Why shouldn't I touch my face?

You might have the virus on your hands after being in a public space and touching door handles and other things. If you touch your face, the virus would be able to enter your body and make you sick.

How do I stop touching my face?

Here are some tips:

- Wash your hands often: it's safe to touch your face if your hands are clean

- Be aware of your emotions: people tend to touch their face less when they're relaxed

- Keep your hands busy: try doing a puzzle, getting a stress ball, or another fun activity

Wear a Mask

×

Why should I wear a mask?

When a person talks, coughs, or sneezes, they release droplets that can carry the virus and make you sick. Masks protect you by blocking these droplets.

It's safest if everyone wears a mask.

By wearing a mask, you're not only protecting yourself but also others around you. This is very important for stopping the spread of Covid-19.

Social Distance

×

What is social distancing?

Social distancing means staying home, staying away from large groups of people, and staying 6 feet (2 meters) away from other people.

Why should we social distance?

It's harder for the virus to spread when people stay far away from each other and interact less.

What about school?

Schools should have communicated with your parents about their plans for this school year. Most schools fit under one of these categories:

- Online learning

- Hybrid (A combination of online and in person)

- In person

Talk to your parents to make sure you know your school's plan and have the supplies you need before the first day of school.
